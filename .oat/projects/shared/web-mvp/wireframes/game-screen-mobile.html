A — Peeking hand (discovery vision)

Board gets maximum height; cards fan up from the bottom edge, partially overlapping the board's bottom rows. Tap your hand to raise it fully (covering more board); tap away to drop it back to a peek. Feels like holding cards at a table.

B — Fixed split

Dedicated hand strip below the board — nothing ever overlaps, every card always fully visible. The board pays for it with ~12% less height. Zero interaction needed to see your hand; most predictable for drag mode.

C — Collapsed fan

Board truly maximized; hand collapses to a small fan in the corner that expands over the board when tapped. Most board real estate, but your hand is hidden by default — costs a tap every single turn, and weakens the "glance at your cards" loop.

All three share the same top player rail: per-player chip color + last-played card (the FR13 requirement), round + timer on the right. Desktop layout is a separate (easier) question — board centered, hand below, rail to the side.
