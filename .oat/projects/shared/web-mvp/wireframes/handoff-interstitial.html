Pass-and-play handoff (local games)

After a move commits in a local game, the device changes hands. What does the incoming player see? (The board is public information either way — the only secret is the hand.)

Tom
Sarah
R4
Tom played 7♦
Pass to Sarah
I'm Sarah — show my hand

A — Full curtain

Classic pass-and-play ritual: the whole screen goes opaque the instant the move commits (board blurred behind). Shows whose turn it is + the last move. Incoming player taps the big button to enter their view. Foolproof; slightly heavier ceremony every single turn.

Tom
Sarah
R4
Tom played 7♦ · your turn, Sarah
Tap to reveal your hand

B — Hand veil only

The board stays fully visible (it's public — both players may study it during the pass). Only the hand area is veiled; the incoming player taps the veil to fan their cards up. Lighter ritual, keeps the game on screen; the veil drops automatically the instant a move commits.

Either way the mechanic is: move commits → outgoing hand hides instantly (no "oops" window) → incoming player explicitly reveals. The choice is ceremony weight.
